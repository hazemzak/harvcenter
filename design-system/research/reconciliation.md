## Competitor pattern (what to avoid)

The entire Cairo سنتر market converges on:
- Gold/amber (#FFBA24–#F5A425) as primary
- Kufam (Arabic cursive) font
- Bootstrap templates with no customization
- Parent-addressed formal Arabic
- Higgsfield AI videos
- Achievement hype language ("أوائل", "تفوق", "متميز")

**Harv differentiates by:** confident red (not gold), Cairo font (modern sans, not cursive), speaking directly to 14–18 year olds, clean compositions with breathing room, real photography only.

## Conflicts resolved

1. **Logo complexity.** The existing logo has graduation cap + stars + sometimes 3D rendering. Decision: keep the cap and stars as part of the core mark but render everything flat — no 3D, no glossy effects, no gradients. The cap, stars, and white interweave lines are integral to the mark's identity.

2. **Gold usage.** Gold appears heavily in current posts for text, borders, stars. Decision: restricted to the logo mark only (stars and tassel at #C9993A). Gold is NOT used in layouts, typography, or borders — the navy + red + white palette remains the foundation for all surfaces. Gold borders and ornamental gold text are still retired.

3. **Campus backgrounds.** Multiple posts use what appears to be Harvard University campus photography as background imagery. Decision: removed. This undermines brand authenticity. Replace with solid navy backgrounds, clean gradients, or real photos of the actual Harv center/classrooms.

4. **Voice audience.** Current posts address parents ("نخبة من أفضل المدرسين", "بيئة تعليمية محفزة للنجاح"). Decision: shift to addressing students directly. Parents still see the content — but the student is the protagonist.

5. **Multiple taglines.** Posts use "معاك للنهاية", "طريقك للتفوق والنجاح", "مستقبلك يبدأ من هنا بثقة!", "مع بعض نحقق الأفضل". Decision: consolidate to **"معاك للنهاية"** as the primary tagline. Others can appear as campaign-specific copy but are not brand-level.
