# Brand summary — Harv

## What Harv is

Harv is an educational center (مركز تعليمي) in Nasr City, Cairo. It primarily prepares students aged 14–18 for thanawiya amma (الثانوية العامة) — the main focus — and also offers baccalaureate preparation. Subjects include French, Italian, Islamic education, sciences, and mathematics. It has operated for years under the name "Harvard Educational Center" and is now modernizing its identity as **Harv**.

## Why it exists

Because exam season in Egypt is brutal, and most students navigate it alone — watching random YouTube videos, cramming from photocopied notes, hoping for the best. Harv gives them a physical place with real teachers who know the curriculum, consistent schedules, and peers going through the same thing.

| Spectrum | Where Harv sits |

## Personality

**Confident. Direct. Warm. Modern. Grounded.**

## Archetype

**The Mentor** — the older brother/sister who already passed the exam and now shows you exactly how. Not a guru on a stage. Not a corporation selling a product. A person across the table who says "I've been where you are."

## Anti-archetype

**The Hype Machine** — 3D-rendered logos, "طريق التفوق والنجاح," AI-generated videos, borrowed prestige from Harvard University, ornamental gold borders. Everything Harv is leaving behind.

## One-sentence promise

Harv is the center where you actually understand the material — not just memorize it.

## Tagline

**معاك للنهاية** — With you till the end.

Alternatives:

- **مكانك هنا** — Your place is here.

## Voice in one paragraph

Harv sounds like the friend who's a year ahead of you in school. Direct, warm, a little funny when it fits. Speaks Egyptian Arabic on social media ("هنراجع مع بعض"), MSA on printed materials. Never talks down to students, never talks over their heads. Uses their words — امتحان, مذاكرة, درجات, ثانوية — not institutional jargon. Every sentence could be spoken out loud without sounding weird.

## The 5-second feeling

A student scrolling Instagram sees a Harv post and feels: **"These people actually get what I'm going through."**

Not impressed. Not sold to. *Understood.*
