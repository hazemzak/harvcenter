# Imagery

## The single most important rule

**Real photography of real teachers and real students in the actual Harv center.**

Not stock. Not AI-generated. Not borrowed from Harvard University's campus. Not generic "education" visuals. The teachers and students ARE the brand — show them.

## Photography

### What we shoot

- **Teachers mid-explanation** — at a whiteboard, holding notes, gesturing. Natural, not posed.
- **Students in session** — writing, listening, focused. Candid over staged.
- **The center itself** — classrooms, entrance, hallways. Clean and well-lit.
- **Teacher portraits** — for social cards and profiles. Clean background (solid color or shallow depth of field). Shoulders up or waist up. Natural expression — a genuine smile, not a corporate pose.
- **Results moments** — students checking scores, celebrating. Authentic reactions only.

### How we shoot

- **Natural light preferred.** If the center has windows, use them. If not, even overhead fluorescent is better than dramatic studio lighting.
- **Phone camera is fine.** A well-composed iPhone photo beats a poorly-lit DSLR shot. Clean the lens.
- **Clean backgrounds.** If the background is cluttered, shoot tighter or find a cleaner wall.
- **Portrait orientation for social** (4:5 for Instagram feed, 9:16 for stories). Landscape for website hero.
- **Minimal editing.** White balance correction, slight exposure adjustment, crop. No heavy filters, no dramatic color grading, no vignette.

### What we don't use

- **Harvard University campus photos.** This is the most important imagery rule. The old posts used what appears to be Harvard campus photography as a backdrop. This stops immediately. It's borrowing prestige from an institution the center has no connection to. It undermines every claim of authenticity.
- **Stock photography of any kind.** No "diverse students studying together." No "happy teenager with backpack." No generic "education" imagery.
- **AI-generated humans.** No Midjourney students, no DALL-E teachers, no Higgsfield AI video characters.
- **AI-generated environments.** No synthetic classrooms, no generated campus shots.
- **3D renders of the logo.** The logo is flat. It exists in 2D. No 3D versions with reflections, metallic textures, or dramatic lighting. The graduation cap and stars in the mark are flat elements — they must never be rendered with 3D depth.
- **Academic imagery clichés.** No stacked books with an apple on top, no pens forming a heart shape, no generic "graduation" stock imagery.

### Image treatments

- **On light backgrounds:** image at full color, radius-lg (16px)
- **On dark backgrounds:** image may have a subtle scrim (linear-gradient overlay from ink-900/50% to transparent) for text readability
- **As teacher card hero:** cut out from background, placed on solid color or clean gradient
- **Never:** filters, duotone, heavy desaturation, color overlays, borders/frames around photos

### What we never do

- Put text directly over a busy photo without a scrim
- Use a photo that's blurry, poorly lit, or obviously stretched
- Use a photo where people are clearly posing for a "corporate team" shot
- Use watermarked stock photos
- Stretch or distort a photo to fit a frame
- Apply Instagram-style filters (Valencia, Clarendon, etc.)

## The teacher card format

This is the most-produced asset at Harv. A teacher card announces a session and features the teacher's photo.

**Acceptable format (the "purple guy" approach):**
- Teacher photo (real, cut out from background or shot on clean backdrop)
- Solid color background (ink-900 navy, brand-600 red, or brand-50 light)
- Clean typography: name + subject + schedule
- Logo mark in corner
- Minimal decorative elements (one or two geometric accents maximum)

**Not acceptable:**
- Teacher photo composited over Harvard campus
- Gold ornamental borders or gold text highlights
- 3D rendered logo behind the teacher
- Multiple competing decorative elements (stars AND borders AND flags AND icons AND gradient text)

## Social image spec

### Instagram feed (1080×1350 — 4:5)
- Safe area: 48px inset from all edges
- Logo mark: top-right or bottom-center, 64px wide
- If text overlay: solid or semi-transparent band behind text

### Instagram story / Reel cover (1080×1920 — 9:16)
- Safe area: 72px from top, 200px from bottom (UI overlay zone)
- Logo mark: top corner, 48px wide

### OG image (1200×630)
- Logo: left-aligned, vertically centered
- Headline: right 60% of the frame
- Background: ink-900 or brand-50

## Patterns and backgrounds

### Acceptable
- **Solid fill:** ink-900, brand-50, paper, surface
- **Subtle grid:** 16×16px dots at ink-200 opacity on paper
- **Brand wash:** soft radial gradient from brand-50 to paper
- **Clean gradient:** ink-900 to ink-700 (dark section backgrounds)

### Not acceptable
- Animated backgrounds
- Particle effects
- Aurora / mesh gradients
- Geometric pattern overlays that compete with content
- Bokeh effects
- Lens flare

## Fallback order when no photography is available

1. **Teacher portrait on solid background** — the minimum viable teacher card
2. **Solid color with typography only** — just the announcement, clean and simple
3. **Icon-based illustration** (Lucide icons composed into a simple graphic) — for concept posts
4. **Never:** AI-generated placeholder photos. If you don't have a photo, design without one. A well-designed text-only post is better than a fake photo.
