# Brand

## Mission

Help high school students in Cairo navigate their most stressful academic years with confidence — by providing focused tutoring, real mentorship, and a space that treats them like adults, not children.

## Vision

Be the center that students actually want to go to — not because their parents made them, but because they know it works and it respects their time.

## Values

1. **Clarity over clutter.** Say it once, say it well. In teaching, in design, in communication.
2. **Respect the student.** They're 14–18, not 6. Talk to them, not about them.
3. **Results you can count.** Every claim has a number behind it. "Our students improved" is nothing. "83% of our ثانوية عامة students scored above 90%" is everything.
4. **Show up consistently.** Same quality every session, every post, every interaction. No hero days followed by ghost weeks.
5. **Real over impressive.** Real teachers, real photos, real results. No borrowed prestige, no AI-generated polish.

## Positioning statement

For high school students in Cairo who are preparing for الثانوية العامة — and بكالوريا — Harv is the educational center that treats you like an adult, delivers results you can measure, and doesn't waste your afternoon with filler. Unlike the dozens of centers that all look the same, talk to your parents, and post the same AI videos — Harv speaks to you, shows up clean, and lets the results do the talking.

## Target audience

### Primary: the student (14–18)

- **Who:** High school students in Cairo (Nasr City and surrounding areas). Primarily الثانوية العامة, also بكالوريا
- **Economic class:** Middle-class families — cost-conscious but willing to invest in education
- **What they care about:** Passing exams with strong scores, not wasting time, being treated with respect, having teachers who actually explain things
- **Where they are:** Instagram (primary), TikTok, WhatsApp groups. NOT Facebook (that's their parents)
- **Insight:** They've been to 3 centers already. They can spot effort vs. laziness in under a second. They share things that look good — and they ignore things that look like every other center's posts.

### Secondary: the parent (35–55)

- **Who:** Mothers and fathers paying for the tutoring
- **What they care about:** Is this center serious? Will my child actually learn? Is it safe? Are the teachers qualified?
- **Where they are:** Facebook, WhatsApp
- **Insight:** They don't need to be sold — they need to be reassured. Clean, professional presentation reassures them more than ornamental gold borders and cluttered layouts.

## Archetype

**The Mentor.** Not the institution, not the guru — the older sibling who already went through it. Calm, competent, direct. Knows the material, knows what the exam actually tests, and doesn't sugarcoat it.

## Anti-archetype

**The Hype Machine.** The center that posts 3D-rendered glossy logos over Harvard campus photos, uses five different fonts per post, promises "طريق التفوق والنجاح" without a single number to back it up, and posts Higgsfield AI videos that look exactly like every other center's Higgsfield AI videos.

## One-sentence promise

We help you pass — and we prove it with numbers, not slogans.
